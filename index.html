Detroit: Become Human

About the Game

Detroit: Become Human is an interactive drama and action-adventure game developed by Quantic Dream. The game is set in a futuristic Detroit in the year 2038, where advanced androids are integrated into society, performing tasks like household chores, factory labor, and assisting the elderly. The story revolves around three main android protagonists Kara, Connor, and Markus who each experience a different path toward self-awareness and autonomy.

Main Characters

Kara: A housekeeping android who fights for survival and humanity.

Connor: A detective android tasked with hunting deviants.

Markus: A caretaker android turned revolutionary leader.

Gameplay and Stakes

Gameplay in Detroit: Become Human centers on decision-making and exploration. Players control the three protagonists, switching between their perspectives throughout the story. The game is heavily driven by choices, with each decision impacting the narrative and leading to various outcomes. Stakes are high as every choice can result in life or death for the characters and others around them. The game emphasizes moral dilemmas, forcing players to weigh loyalty, survival, and ethics in their decisions.

The Narrative Arc

The story begins with androids firmly under human control. Kara escapes an abusive owner to protect a young girl, Connor is an advanced detective android hunting deviant androids, and Markus becomes a revolutionary leader fighting for android rights. Over time, these characters grapple with themes of freedom, identity, and morality. The narrative builds to a climax as Markus’ revolution, Connor’s investigation, and Kara’s journey for safety converge, creating an emotionally charged tapestry of interconnected stories.

Endings

The game offers multiple endings depending on the player’s choices. Outcomes range from peaceful coexistence between humans and androids to violent rebellion or the complete failure of the androids’ quest for autonomy. Each ending reflects the cumulative impact of decisions made throughout the game, providing a deeply personal conclusion for each player.

Conclusion

Detroit: Become Human is a compelling exploration of humanity, morality, and free will. Its narrative-driven gameplay and emotional depth make it both entertaining and thought-provoking. The game's resonance with Brian Massumi’s concept of affect—as described in "The Autonomy of Affect"—adds an intellectual dimension, showcasing how emotions and instinctual responses shape the characters’ paths to self-awareness. For me, the game is not just entertaining but also a powerful reflection on societal issues like discrimination and the fight for equality.
